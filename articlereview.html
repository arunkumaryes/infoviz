Interaction & Narrative Design
The article focuses on the mess created by humans on earth's orbit. By highlighting China's activities in space, the author establishes comparative and relative strategies to make sense to the viewer and show the magnitude of the problem with visual aids
Arun Kumar S
The article opens up by giving a brief note of space debris in Low Earth Orbit (LEO) and mentions its dangers for space station and satellites floating around. As the article is published from geographic region of China, its mentioned they are one of the many responsible for this space dump
Infographic of space debris belongs to China are shown in a macro scale with layers of different kinds of debris (reveal)
The color Red is used as both depiction of China's flag color and as a danger color. Operating satellite are added an extra tint of red
Then we get a dropdown option to choose a country or space agency to know its space junk. When selecting one, the numbers appear with a counting effect
Upon choosing each country, the debris is depicted with a contextual graphic on why a particular crash occurred, respect to the country. The color red is given to China to maintain the relevance (conventional break)
The dropdown can be made as buttons with different countries and comparison can be added
The details are changed from macro to micro, explaining how the debris happens, by quoting China's deliberate and accidental disasters
Its another level of scale transition where we see what's the kind of debris floating around, with respect to each year
Now comes the WHY? part. This graphic explains the impact of space debris in relation to the scenarios/objects that we can simulate and understand
The narrative became a bit lengthy, as the author tried to cover all possible data he got. Here the color red is used for other countries, which is inconsistent
When the general viewers makes it up till this part, a sudden horizontal scroll timeline with history of China's space launches is introduced. The graphic scrolls horizontally if we scroll vertically down. This provides a good insights to the people who are interested in this subject
This graphic forther explains the measures taken by space agencies to grab the space junk
